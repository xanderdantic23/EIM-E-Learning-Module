EIM E-LEARNING MODULE

Greetings and salutations from the Electrical Installation and Maintenance (EIM) Online Learning Module! With this lesson, you will gain a thorough grasp of the tools that are frequently used for electrical installation and maintenance operations. Regardless of your level of experience, this module will walk you through the key tools and their features, whether you are just starting out in the industry or a seasoned professional wanting to brush up on your knowledge.

Hummer

For example, hammers are used for general carpentry, framing, nail pulling, cabinet making, assembling furniture, upholstering, finishing, riveting, bending or shaping metal, striking masonry drills and steel chisels, and so on.

Phillips Screwdriver

The Phillips screwdriver is mostly used in the automotive industry and it has a cross (X) shape at the tip. This type of tool is used to turn screws with more torque to achieve the desired fit and is less prone to slippage.

Flat screw

Flat screws are versatile and are often used in various applications, including woodworking, construction, and general household tasks.

Allen-Screwdriver

An Allen screwdriver, also known as a hex screwdriver or hex key, is designed for driving hexagonal socket screws, widely used in furniture assembly, machinery, automotive, and electronics for secure, low-profile connections.

Lineman's Pliers

Overall, Lineman's pliers are a multi-functional tool that combines cutting, gripping, crimping, and shaping capabilities, making them indispensable for professionals working in electrical and utility industries.

Long Nose

Long-nose pliers are commonly used in various industries, including electronics, electrical work, jewelry making, and general precision tasks. They are an essential tool for situations that require a fine touch and reach into tight spaces.

Side Cutting

Side-cutting pliers are an essential tool for electricians, technicians, and anyone working with wires, providing a convenient and effective way to cut and trim various types of materials.

Wire Scripper

wire strippers are essential tools in electrical work, electronics assembly, and other applications where precision and control in removing insulation from wires are crucial for creating reliable and safe electrical connections

Circuit Breaker

Overall, the primary purpose of a circuit breaker is to enhance the safety and reliability of electrical systems by protecting against overcurrents and electrical faults. They are crucial components in residential, commercial, and industrial electrical installations.

Switch

the primary purpose of a switch is to control the flow of electrical current, allowing users to turn devices on or off, providing safety, and enabling manual or automated control of electrical systems. Switches come in various types and configurations, each suited to specific applications and requirements.

Flush type

The purpose of using flush-type fasteners is primarily aesthetic. They create a smooth and even surface, reducing the risk of snagging, catching, or interference.

Surface type

The purpose of surface-type fasteners or mounts is to provide a visible and accessible means of attachment. This can be relevant in construction or manufacturing where appearance or the ability to easily remove or adjust components is important.

Lamp Holder

Overall, the purpose of a lamp holder is to facilitate the connection and support of light bulbs in lighting fixtures, enabling the illumination of spaces in homes, offices, and various other environments.

Junction

An enclosure used to contain electrical connections, providing protection and organization for wires.

Utility box

Similar to a junction box, it's used to house electrical connections but may be larger and designed for outdoor or industrial use.

Angle Grinder

A handheld power tool used for cutting, grinding, and polishing materials such as metal, stone, or concrete.

Auger brace

A manual tool used for drilling holes in wood, typically with a rotating bit operated by turning a handle.

VBall peen hammer

A type of hammer with a rounded head on one side and a flat striking surface on the other, used for shaping metal and driving punches.

Clamps

Various types of devices used to hold objects together or secure them in place during work, often used in woodworking and metalworking.

Electrician's Knife

A specialized knife designed for cutting electrical insulation and stripping wire.

EMT bender

A tool used to bend electrical metallic tubing (EMT) into desired shapes for routing electrical wiring.

Hand Drill

A manually operated tool for drilling holes in various materials by rotating a bit with a crank or handle.

Heat Gun

A device that emits a stream of hot air, used for tasks such as shrinking heat shrink tubing, removing paint, and softening adhesives.

Hydraulic Bender

A tool used to bend metal pipes or conduits using hydraulic pressure, often used in plumbing and electrical work.

Mallet

A type of hammer with a large, typically rubber or wooden head, used for striking objects without damaging them.

Soldering tools

Equipment used for soldering, including a soldering iron, solder, flux, and other accessories for joining metal parts together.

Solid Wire

Electrical wire with a single solid conductor, commonly used in residential wiring.

Steel tape

A measuring tape made of steel, used for taking accurate measurements in construction and other applications.

Stranded Wire

Electrical wire made of multiple small strands twisted or braided together, providing flexibility and durability.

Vernier caliper

A precision measuring tool used to accurately measure small distances, thicknesses, and diameters.

Voltmeter

A device used to measure electrical voltage in a circuit, typically with probes that are placed across the component or circuit being tested.
